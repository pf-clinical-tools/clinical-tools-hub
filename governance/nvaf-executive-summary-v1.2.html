Anticoagulation Risk Assessment Tool in NVAF

Executive Summary (Version 1.2)

Author: Dr Prabhath Fernando MBBS MD FRCP
Associated digital tool: Anticoagulation Risk Assessment Tool in Non-Valvular Atrial Fibrillation (NVAF)
Current version: v1.2 (frozen)
Intended users: Clinicians involved in the management of atrial fibrillation
Intended setting: NHS secondary care and community practice

Purpose

The Anticoagulation Risk Assessment Tool in NVAF is a clinician-facing digital decision support tool designed to assist structured, transparent decision-making regarding oral anticoagulation in patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation.

The tool aims to support clinicians in balancing stroke prevention against bleeding risk, particularly in older adults and patients with frailty, where decisions are frequently complex, preference-sensitive, and inadequately represented by trial populations.

Clinical Rationale

Atrial fibrillation is a major risk factor for ischaemic stroke, and oral anticoagulation significantly reduces this risk. However, anticoagulation also confers a risk of major bleeding. In older and frail patients, both stroke and bleeding may have disproportionately severe consequences, and competing risks, comorbidity, and patient priorities often influence decision-making.

While clinical guidelines recommend assessment of both stroke and bleeding risk, these are commonly considered separately or inconsistently in practice. This tool presents validated stroke and bleeding risk estimates side-by-side, supporting coherent clinical reasoning rather than algorithmic decision-making.

Evidence Base

The tool uses two validated, guideline-endorsed risk scores without modification:

  • CHA₂DS₂-VASc to estimate annual stroke risk without anticoagulation
  • ORBIT to estimate annual major bleeding risk with anticoagulation

ORBIT was selected in preference to HAS-BLED due to its reliance on objective variables, strong predictive performance for major bleeding, and applicability to contemporary anticoagulation practice.

Risk estimates presented are derived from population-level cohort data and are expressed as approximate annual risks.

Design and Safety Features

Key safety-by-design features include:

  • No automated treatment recommendations
  • Conservative, non-directive language
  • Explicit signalling of uncertainty (e.g. Net clinical benefit: uncertain; individualise)
  • Internal consistency checks to prevent contradictory age band selection
  • Reset functionality to reduce risk of cross-patient data contamination
  • Clear statements that the tool supports, but does not replace, clinical judgement

The tool deliberately avoids generating a single numerical net benefit score, reflecting the absence of validated methods to combine stroke and bleeding scores into an individualised predictive metric.

Intended Use

The tool is intended to:

  • Support clinicianpatient discussions
  • Promote consistent, auditable reasoning
  • Reduce unwarranted variation in practice
  • Assist documentation of decision-making, particularly in cases of uncertainty

It is particularly useful in patients with intermediate stroke risk, elevated bleeding risk, frailty, or prior bleeding.

Limitations

  • Risk estimates are population-based and do not predict individual outcomes
  • Frailty and competing risks are not fully captured by existing risk scores
  • The tool does not replace specialist input or shared decision-making

These limitations are explicitly acknowledged within the tool.

Governance and Version Control

Version v1.2 of the NVAF Anticoagulation Risk Assessment Tool is frozen.
Further changes will only be made in response to:

  • National or international guideline updates
  • New high-quality evidence
  • Identified safety issues

The tool is supported by a full Governance & Evidence document (v1.2) and is suitable for use within NHS clinical governance frameworks.
